Chapter 18: Feeding AI, Healing the Organisation

Once an organisation is decomposed into autonomous business units, a new question becomes unavoidable: how does the organisation remain coherent without reverting to coordination as a surrogate for ownership?

The answer is synthesis. But synthesis of a specific kind.

In software-dependent corporates, coherence has traditionally been maintained through meetings, escalation paths, programme management, and social negotiation. These mechanisms are expensive, fragile, and deeply dependent on individual memory. They are also the primary reason illusion scales more easily than reality: social coherence can be maintained through narrative long after structural coherence has collapsed.

Autonomous business units remove the need for much of this coordination, but they do not remove the need for shared understanding. Units must know what other units promise and what they require. The organisation must know whether its stated strategy is reflected in the behaviour of its systems. Leadership must know where drift is occurring, where contracts are being violated, and where the system is diverging from intent.
